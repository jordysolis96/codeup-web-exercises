National Parks FAQ

Expand All Collapse All
1. How many people visit the national parks?

Total recreation visitors to the national parks in 2019: 327,516,619.

2. What is the largest national park site? Smallest?

Largest: Wrangell-St. Elias National Park and Preserve, AK, at 13.2 million acres Smallest: Thaddeus Kosciuszko National Memorial, PA, at 0.02 acres

3. What do I need to know about driving off road in national parks?

Before you head out, check with the national parks that you intend to visit. In many national parks, off-road driving is illegal. Where off-road driving is allowed, the National Park Service regulates it.

4. Can I bring my pet to a national park?

Some national parks welcome pets—in developed areas, on many trails and campgrounds, and in some lodging facilities.

5. How many areas are in the National Park System?

The system includes 419 areas covering more than 85 million acres in every state, the District of Columbia, American Samoa, Guam, Puerto Rico, and the Virgin Islands. These areas include national parks, monuments, battlefields, military parks, historical parks, historic sites, lakeshores, seashores, recreation areas, scenic rivers and trails, and the White House.

6. How old is the National Park System?

The National Park Service was created by an act signed by President Woodrow Wilson on August 25, 1916. Yellowstone National Park was established by an act signed by President Ulysses S. Grant on March 1, 1872, as the nation's first national park.

7. How many are "national parks"?

Out of the 419 parks in the National Park System in 2019, there were 62 with the designation "national park" as part of their official name.

8. What is the most visited "national park"?

In 2019, Great Smoky Mountains National Park received 12.5 million recreational visits. Grand Canyon National park ranked second with 5.97 million recreational visits.

9. What is the most visited park in the National Park System?

In 2019, Golden Gate National Recreation Area received 15 million recreational visits.

10. How many employees are in the National Park Service?

Permanent, temporary, and seasonal employees: Approximately 20,000 Volunteers: More than 279,000 in 2019

Yellowstone National Park

  • The park recorded more than 4 million visits in 2019; down 2.3% from 2018
  • Yellowstone National Park was established on March 1, 1872
  • Yellowstone is the world’s first national park
  • Larger than Rhode Island and Delaware combined

Grand Canyon National Park

  • Grand Canyon did not become a national park until 1919
  • Covers an area of 1,900 square miles
  • Is 190 miles long
  • Contains some 277 miles of the Colorado River is populated by five Indian tribes: The Hopi, Navajo, Havasupai, Paiute, and Hualapai

Hot Springs National Park

  • Hot Springs National Park covers a total area of 5,550 acres
  • The number of people visiting Hot Springs in 2019 was 1,467,153
  • Hot Springs was made a national park on March 4, 1921
  • The spring water flowing from Hot Springs Mountain is quite safe to drink.
